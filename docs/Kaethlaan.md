# Sigil — The Kingdom of Kaethlaan

### v0.4 Lore Document — Foundation Set

Kaethlaan is the first of the six kingdoms of the Sigil world. It is the starting realm — the hero-kingdom, the lit hearth at the edge of dark woods. This document defines its culture, institutions, geography, and cosmology, and establishes the conventions that future kingdom sets will be designed against.

This v0.4 update reflects the v0.6 ruleset's rename of the *Shadow* element to *Dark* and the locking-in of the Arlia card roster.

-----

## The World at a Glance

The Sigil world is composed of **six kingdoms**. Each future set will expand the game into one new kingdom, with its own institutions, factions, and signature characters. Beloved characters may appear across multiple kingdoms as **alternate versions** — same soul, different homeland, different stats and affiliations. Arlia is a young Kaethlaani trainee in this set; she may be something else entirely if born elsewhere.

Between and beyond the kingdoms lie the wild places — creature camps, monster nests, and unaffiliated dangers that exist outside any kingdom's politics.

-----

## Kaethlaan

### Identity

Kaethlaan is the classic-fantasy hero-kingdom. Developed. Generally benevolent. Well-ordered. The kind of place a young hero is born and trained before adventuring outward. From the outside, it looks like everything good fantasy promises: a wise king, honorable knights, learned mages, prosperous towns, and a clear sense of right.

But Kaethlaan is not a paradise. **Inside its institutions live deeper factions with deeper goals.** The kingdom's apparatus of identifying and cultivating talent is not as innocent as official doctrine suggests. The kingdom that means well still asks too much of the people it raises — its oaths, its orders, its programs of cultivation each exact a quiet cost from those they elevate. This is the central tension Kaethlaan offers as a story-engine: the tragedy of an honorable system that nevertheless breaks the people it loves.

### Colors

**Blue, teal, and silver.** Clear sky, deep water, and well-kept steel. These appear on banners, uniforms, ceremonial gear, and the heraldry of the Crown.

### Climate and Geography

**Temperate.** Four seasons. Forested borders, cultivated central lands, towns and roads connecting major institutions. The wild places — where the creature camps live — surround the kingdom's edges.

### Rulership

**King Honathan of Kaethlaan** rules the kingdom in the current era. He is a playable card and may be elevated to Leader from your active or passive zone during the opening phase (per v0.3 Leader elevation rules). A future card, **Honathan the Great**, will depict his younger warrior-king self — a different stat line, a different affiliation profile, and a meaningfully different role in a deck. This establishes the precedent for "same character across time" as a design pattern.

## Institutions of Kaethlaan

### The Kaethlaan Knights

The kingdom's martial body. Sworn defenders of the realm. The Knights operate as an order with a strong oath-culture — vows are real, binding, and culturally weighty. A Squire trains, takes the Vow to become a Dame or Sir Knight, and may rise eventually to Captain and beyond.

The Knights wear Kaethlaan's colors. Their banners are carried into every battle, and the loss of a banner is a wound felt by every Knight under it. The Knights provide the kingdom's most visible heroes — and many of its most public tragedies.

**Affiliation tag:** *The Kaethlaan Knights*

### The Mages Guild

The kingdom's institution of magical learning. Magic in Kaethlaan is **accepted, organized, and taught**. A young mage joins the Guild, trains in the elements, and rises through study and demonstrated mastery. The Guild's existence as a respected body — rather than a feared or persecuted one — is one of the things that marks Kaethlaan as a developed kingdom. Other kingdoms may handle magic very differently.

The Guild teaches the six elements (see Cosmology below). Specialization is encouraged; mastery of multiple elements is rare and prestigious.

**Affiliation tag:** *The Mages Guild*

### The Archive

The inner echelon of the Mages Guild. Senior scholars, archmages, and keepers of dangerous knowledge belong to The Archive. A character is a member of the Guild from the moment they begin training; they only join The Archive when they reach the highest tier of accomplishment.

A character may carry both affiliations (Mages Guild AND Archive) simultaneously — Archive membership is additive, not a replacement.

**Affiliation tag:** *The Archive*

### King's Court (Kaethlaan)

The inner political circle around King Honathan. Distinct from the Knights (who serve the realm martially) and the Guild (which serves the realm magically), the King's Court is the body of advisors, captains, and trusted figures who personally counsel and serve the Crown. Membership is conferred by the King's favor, not by examination or oath, and is therefore narrow and prestigious. Captains of the Royal Army who have earned the King's personal trust commonly hold this affiliation; so do senior Archmages who advise on matters of state.

**Affiliation tag:** *King's Court (Kaethlaan)*

### Future Institutions

Additional Kaethlaani affiliations will be introduced as new characters require them. Some may emerge as inner chapters of existing bodies; others may be parallel institutions (merchant leagues, religious orders, regional militias). Houses (noble families) are deliberately shelved for now and will be introduced when a character needs one.

## The Wild Borders

Surrounding Kaethlaan are the unaffiliated wild places — home to creatures and beasts. These exist outside the kingdom's politics and serve as the game's filler and opposition layer.

Under the v0.3 ruleset's **unified character/creature tier ladder**, creatures are not a separate class. They are characters with shorter arcs. The previous v0.1 distinction between "Beasts" (no progression) and "Tribal Creatures" (one upgrade) has been dissolved in favor of a single schema where every entity has a terminal tier appropriate to their design.

In practice:

- **A wild thestral** is a T1-terminal character. Single card. No progression. Functions as filler or opposition.
- **Lor'oak Goblin Grunt → Lor'oak Goblin Commander** is a T2-terminal character. Two cards. One upgrade. A tribal creature with light progression.
- A more elaborate creature warband leader might be a T3-terminal character with two upgrades.

The same tier-bonus structure applies to creatures as to Kingdom characters. **This means a creature can be elevated to Leader.** A goblin warband deck might run Lor'oak Goblin Commander as its designated Leader, with the corresponding +30 tier bonus (since he's T2). Niche decks are a feature of the system, not a bug — the kingdom layer and the wild layer share the same mechanics.

The textural layering still holds in terms of *design intent*:

1. **Single-form wild creatures** serve as cheap, simple opposition and filler
1. **Two- or three-form tribal creatures** offer light-progression decks with simple synergies
1. **Kingdom characters** offer the deepest progression, falls, terminals, and narrative arcs

But these layers are gradations of complexity on a unified schema, not separate categories with separate rules.

## Cosmology — Elements and Light/Dark

### The Six Elements

**Fire, Water, Earth, Wind, Light, Dark.**

Elements are **nature itself** — they exist everywhere, in every kingdom, regardless of culture. They are not a Kaethlaani invention; they are the substance of magic across the world. What varies by kingdom is *how magic is approached* (the Mages Guild's elemental tradition is Kaethlaan's approach; other kingdoms may have non-elemental traditions like blood magic, name magic, or pact magic).

### Elemental Matchups

The physical elements form a classic rock-paper-scissors cycle:

**Fire > Earth > Wind > Water > Fire**

- Fire deals +10 damage against Earth on attack
- Earth deals +10 damage against Wind on attack
- Wind deals +10 damage against Water on attack
- Water deals +10 damage against Fire on attack

**Light and Dark are neutral to all four physical elements.** They do not amplify against or take extra damage from Fire/Water/Earth/Wind. They interact only with each other:

- Light deals +10 damage to Dark on attack
- Dark ignores the first DEF check per turn against Light targets

### Light and Dark — The Cosmological Pair

Light and Dark are different from the other four elements. They are both **elements** (studied at the Guild like any other) and **trajectories** (cosmological states a character can fall into). This double meaning is intentional and central to Sigil's identity.

**Dark is what answers when the world asks too much.**

- Some characters come to Dark through the **studied door** — they enroll in the Guild, train in Dark magic, and learn its disciplines (necromancy-adjacent arts, illusion, void, entropy). They are not evil. They are mages who chose a difficult, sometimes feared specialization. They remain morally normal members of society.
- Other characters come to Dark through the **suffered door** — they were Light-element heroes who were broken by tragedy, betrayal, or systemic failure. Their *trajectory* darkens. Their *being* shifts. They become Dark not because they studied it, but because the world made them Dark.
- A third path exists: characters who reach Dark through a **chosen branch** — not breakage but a deliberate turn taken, often via disillusionment with the institutional path they were on. They walk into Dark with eyes open. (Arlia's wanderer-line, culminating in The Ascended, follows this path: she is a Fire mage who steps off the Guild's ladder, drifts into Light as the Wandering Acolyte, then transcends into a Dark-and-Light hybrid at her apex.)
- **A few characters come through more than one door.** These are among the most dangerous figures in the world. (Design space reserved for future cards.)

**Light has the parallel structure.**

- Studied: mages who specialize in Light magic — amplification, healing, revelation.
- Embodied: heroes raised by the kingdom's institutions in the path the world wrote for them.
- Both, occasionally: figures whose institutional path and studied discipline align fully.

### Light vs Dark Interaction

Light and Dark are opposed *as trajectories*, not as morals. The opposition is real in mechanical terms (+10 damage Light→Dark; Dark ignores first DEF check vs Light). They are not good and evil — they are *the path the world wrote* and *the path the world breaks or turns people into*.

## Element Distribution in Kaethlaan

Kaethlaan, as a temperate hero-kingdom with the Mages Guild as a respected institution, sees **all six elements taught and practiced openly.** No element is forbidden. Dark is taught alongside Light without stigma in the Guild's halls — the discipline is respected even if its public reputation is more cautious.

Most Kaethlaani Light characters are heroes-in-trajectory (the institutional path). Most Kaethlaani Dark mages came through the studied door and are normal members of society. **Characters whose Light has darkened into Dark** — those broken into the trajectory rather than choosing it — are rarer, more dramatic, and culturally treated with grief rather than condemnation.

Other kingdoms may have very different elemental cultures. Some may forbid certain elements. Some may revere one above others. Some may have no elemental tradition at all.

## Affiliation Architecture

Affiliations in Sigil exist at three **cultural** scopes — describing the reach of who can belong to them:

1. **Kingdom-internal** — bodies that exist only within one kingdom. *The Kaethlaan Knights, The Mages Guild, The Archive, King's Court (Kaethlaan).* These are political and cultural memberships.
1. **Cross-border / regional** — bodies that span multiple kingdoms but not the whole world. (None defined yet; will emerge as future kingdoms are designed and inter-kingdom factions appear.)
1. **Universal** — affiliations that transcend kingdoms entirely. These are usually *states of being* rather than memberships. *The Forsaken* (those broken beyond return), *The Returned* (those who have come back from death), *The Faithless* (those whose faith in their path has been irrecoverably shattered), *The Ascended* (those who have transcended mortal limits), *The Divine Channel* (those who commune directly with gods). These are cosmological-level categories that any kingdom's characters can fall, choose, or transcend into.

The Universal affiliations are what give the Sigil world its **shared cosmological floor.** No matter which kingdom a character is from, tragedy speaks the same language, and Dark has the same source.

Cultural scope is distinct from **mechanical scope** — how a card's text reaches across the table. By default, when a card references an affiliation in its text, the reference applies to characters on the controller's side only; a **(G) suffix** opts in to global (cross-side) reach. See the Ruleset for the mechanical details.

## World-State Cards and Kaethlaan

The v0.3 ruleset introduces **field cards** as a category — cards that play into a passive slot and alter the world-state. They split into two scopes:

- **World-state field cards** (cosmic in scope, affecting both sides of the board): natural disasters, divine acts, cosmic events. *Plague* is the canonical example — a world-state card that damages all characters and serves as a transformation trigger for fall-path characters.
- **Kingdom field cards** (your kingdom's investment, affecting only your side): institutional efforts, kingdom-specific magic, focused research. *Medical Advancement* is the canonical example — a Kaethlaani kingdom card that heals your characters and incidentally nullifies the HP damage from Plague (without naming Plague, per Mathematical Opposition).

Kaethlaan, as a developed kingdom with strong institutions, will have many **Kingdom field cards** representing its investments: Medical Advancement, Royal Decree, Coronation, Festival, Knight Training Programs. These are signature cards of a kingdom that *organizes* its responses to the world.

Other kingdoms may emphasize different card categories. A kingdom with weaker institutions might lean on world-state cards (its citizens are buffeted by cosmic forces it can't channel). A kingdom organized around individual heroes might have few field cards at all.

## Design Principles for Kaethlaan Cards

When designing characters, equipment, events, and field cards that belong to Kaethlaan, the following principles apply:

1. **Visual identity.** Kaethlaani cards lean toward blue, teal, and silver in their art and frame treatments.
1. **Institutional voice.** Kaethlaani flavor text and ability names reflect the kingdom's developed, organized character. Vows, orders, programs, ranks, ceremonies. Magic is *studied*, not raw.
1. **Tragic depth under benevolent surface.** Kaethlaani transformations onto Dark — whether falls or chosen branches — should feel like *the kingdom's failure to protect or hold its own*, not like external corruption. The darkness comes from within.
1. **Elemental openness.** All six elements appear in Kaethlaani characters. Light-trajectory heroes are common; Dark-trajectory turns are dramatic. Studied-Dark mages exist alongside both without cultural shame.
1. **Universal affiliations carry weight.** When a Kaethlaani character enters The Forsaken, The Returned, The Ascended, or The Divine Channel, that crossing matters. The Kingdom doesn't *own* such characters anymore — they belong to the cosmological layer.
1. **Kingdom field cards as institutional response.** Kaethlaan's organized character is reflected in its field-card category: it has many Kingdom-scope cards representing its investments and structures. (Per the Mathematical Opposition principle, these cards do not directly name what they counter; their effects emerge from the math.)

## Open Threads for Future Sets

These are things established in Kaethlaan that future kingdom sets will need to reckon with:

- **What do the other five kingdoms do with magic?** Each should have a distinct tradition. Possible directions: blood magic, name magic, pact/contract magic, ritual magic, divine magic, no magic at all.
- **What do other kingdoms do for prodigy identification and cultivation?** Some may have nothing equivalent. Some may have darker versions. Some may have *better* versions. (The Destined remains as an unanchored affiliation tag in the world; this is one possible home for it.)
- **What are the inter-kingdom relationships?** Wars, alliances, trade, exile — to be defined as kingdoms are added.
- **What cross-border affiliations emerge?** These will appear naturally as multi-kingdom characters or organizations are designed.
- **What are the unaffiliated wild places between kingdoms?** Creature camps are established for Kaethlaan's borders; the deeper wilds and the spaces between kingdoms remain to be defined.
- **What field cards belong to each kingdom?** Kaethlaan's institutional voice produces many Kingdom-scope cards. Other kingdoms may produce more world-state cards or none at all.

## Locked Decisions (Reference)

For quick reference, this set locks in the following decisions:

- **Six kingdoms total. Kaethlaan is the first.**
- **Kaethlaan is the hero-kingdom: developed, benevolent, blue/teal/silver, temperate, ruled by King Honathan.**
- **The Kaethlaan Knights** is the kingdom's martial body.
- **The Mages Guild** is the kingdom's magical institution. **The Archive** is its senior inner echelon.
- **King's Court (Kaethlaan)** is the inner political circle around the Crown — narrow, conferred by royal favor.
- **House Vael and other noble houses are deferred** to future design as needed.
- **Six elements exist universally as nature.** Fire, Water, Earth, Wind, Light, Dark.
- **Elemental matchups:** Fire > Earth > Wind > Water > Fire. Light and Dark are neutral to physical elements and opposed only to each other.
- **Light and Dark carry double meaning** — element AND trajectory. Dark has the studied door, the suffered door, and the chosen branch.
- **Hybrid elements are admitted.** A character may print a compound element (e.g., *Dark & Light* on The Ascended).
- **Creatures are characters with shorter arcs.** The Beast/Tribal Creature distinction from v0.1 is dissolved. All entities are on the unified tier ladder, with terminal tiers ranging from T1 (single-card beasts) to T4 (long-arc legendary characters like Arlia).
- **Any character can be elevated to Leader.** A goblin warband may run Lor'oak Goblin Commander as its designated Leader. Niche decks are valid design space.
- **Universal affiliations** (The Forsaken, The Returned, The Faithless, The Ascended, The Divine Channel) operate above the kingdom layer.
- **King Honathan of Kaethlaan** is the current ruler. **Honathan the Great** (his younger self) is a future card and establishes the "same character across time" pattern.
- **Kingdom field cards** are a signature Kaethlaani card category, representing the kingdom's institutional investments. They affect only your side and emerge from organized response to the world.

### What changed from v0.1

- **Beast/Tribal Creature distinction dissolved.** v0.1's three-layer textural model (Beasts / Tribal Creatures / Kingdom Characters) is replaced with a single unified tier ladder where every entity has a terminal tier. Creatures are characters with shorter arcs.
- **Added "Kaethlaan and World-State Cards" section** introducing field cards as a category and Kaethlaan's signature Kingdom-scope card type.
- **Updated rulership section** to reference the v0.3 Leader elevation system.
- **Removed retired-name notes.** The Crimson Order → Kaethlaan Knights rename was a v0.1 concern; v0.2 simply uses the current names without retrospective references.
- **Added "Any character can be elevated to Leader"** to locked decisions, reflecting the v0.3 Leader system and goblin-leader-deck validity.

### What changed from v0.2

- **The Destined retired as a Kaethlaan-internal institution.** Removed from the institutions section, the affiliation-architecture list, and locked decisions. The tag remains valid as a world-level affiliation with scope to be defined later (cross-border, universal, or kingdom-internal-to-some-other-kingdom — TBD).
- **Identity section rerouted.** The tragic-engine theme no longer leans on The Destined specifically. The kingdom's institutional cost is now carried by its oaths, orders, and programs generally rather than by one named program.
- **Open Threads question generalized.** The "What do other kingdoms do with Destined-like programs?" prompt is now phrased about prodigy identification generally, with The Destined noted as a possible future home.
- **Base tier collapsed into T1** at the ruleset level (v0.5). No direct Kaethlaan lore changes needed — the kingdom's tier ladder now starts at T1 in line with the rest of the world.

### What changed from v0.3

- **Shadow element renamed to Dark, globally.** Every reference in this document — element list, matchup chart, cosmological-pair framing, distribution discussion, design principles — now reads *Dark*. The studied-door / suffered-door framing and the Light/Dark-as-trajectory double meaning are preserved unchanged in substance; only the word *Shadow* has been retired.
- **Chosen-branch path added to the Dark trajectory framing.** A third door exists alongside studied and suffered: characters who turn into Dark deliberately, often via disillusionment with their institutional path. Arlia's wanderer-line is the canonical example; she is not broken into Dark, she walks into it.
- **King's Court (Kaethlaan) added as an institution.** Inner political circle around the King; conferred by royal favor; narrow and prestigious. Captain Arlia of the Royal Army is the first character to hold this affiliation.
- **The Ascended and The Divine Channel added as Universal affiliations.** Both cosmological-level categories that any kingdom's characters can enter; both first appearing on Arlia's terminal form, The Ascended.
- **Hybrid elements admitted.** Locked decisions list updated to note that a character may print a compound element (Dark & Light on The Ascended).
- **Affiliation Architecture section** clarifies the distinction between cultural scope (the v0.3 three-tier classification) and mechanical scope (the (G)-suffix rule introduced in Ruleset v0.6). Lore retains the cultural-scope framing.
